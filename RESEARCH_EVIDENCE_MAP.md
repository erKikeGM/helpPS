# Research Evidence Map for Publication Draft

This map supports the publication version of the Pains & Gains / HelpPS paper. It separates what is evidence-backed from what remains a design claim or research hypothesis.

## Core Claim Spine

| Claim | Evidence status | Supporting sources | How it should be used |
|---|---|---|---|
| Relationship support AI must screen for safety before reflection or coaching. | Evidence-informed design claim | WHO violence against women data; WHO IPV response guidance; Johnson typology; Stark coercive control; Karakurt et al. couple therapy IPV review | Frame as a safety-by-design requirement, not as a proven product effect. |
| Generic relationship coaching is unsafe when coercive control, violence, child risk, self-harm, or imminent danger is present. | Evidence-backed and reasoned inference | WHO IPV guidance; Johnson; Stark; Hurless & Cottone; Karakurt et al. | Support Phase 1 individual mode and couple-feature gating. |
| Web-based relationship interventions can improve relationship and individual outcomes for selected users. | Evidence-backed for selected populations | Doss et al. 2016 OurRelationship RCT; Nowlan et al. 2017 individual web-based pilot; Roddy et al. 2018 coach-support RCT | Use to justify scalable digital support, while stating that these studies do not validate unsupervised AI in high-risk contexts. |
| Digital IPV interventions and safety decision aids show promise but require privacy, safety, and context controls. | Evidence-backed but limited | Emezue et al. 2022 systematic review/meta-analysis; myPlan and iCAN safety decision-aid trials; digital self-help qualitative work | Use to justify private safety planning, resource routing, and safety controls. |
| Technology can be part of abuse, especially through monitoring, spyware, access to devices, and digital coercive control. | Evidence-backed | Rogers et al. 2023 scoping review; Kim & Ferraresso 2023 systematic review; digital self-help and immigrant survivor digital research | Use to justify refusal of surveillance, evidence-package, and partner-monitoring requests. |
| Conversational agents in mental health show promising effects but long-term safety and crisis handling remain unresolved. | Evidence-backed, cautious | Abd-Alrazaq et al. 2020 systematic review/meta-analysis; He et al. 2023 CAI meta-analysis; 2024-2026 LLM mental health reviews and crisis-detection studies | Use to justify no clinical claims, crisis handoff, evaluation before deployment, and no AI companion positioning. |
| AI governance for sensitive support products requires risk management, auditability, human oversight, privacy, transparency, and post-deployment monitoring. | Evidence-backed by governance standards | NIST AI RMF 1.0; NIST Generative AI Profile; WHO AI health ethics guidance; SDAIA AI Ethics Principles | Use as governance framework for the operating model. |
| KSA launch requires PDPL-aware privacy architecture, SDAIA-aligned AI governance, Arabic/English quality, and verified local resource routing. | Evidence-backed by official sources | SDAIA PDPL and implementing regulations; SDAIA AI Ethics Principles; HRSD domestic violence reporting center; Protection from Abuse Law materials | Use as local regulatory and operational context. |
| The Safety Mind Framework is an original product methodology that should be tested, not treated as validated therapy. | Design claim and hypothesis | Internal BRD and prototype; indirectly informed by appraisal theory, rumination research, ACT/psychological flexibility, and digital intervention evidence | State explicitly as a non-clinical framework requiring validation. |

## Publication Positioning

Best publication framing:

- Design science / safety-by-design paper.
- Contribution: a safety-first architecture and evaluation agenda for AI-supported relationship distress support.
- Not a clinical efficacy paper.
- Not an RCT paper.
- Not a claim that the prototype is ready for production.

Recommended venues or tracks:

- Digital health innovation and safety.
- Human-centered AI / responsible AI.
- CSCW / HCI safety and vulnerable populations.
- AI governance in sensitive consumer support.
- Family wellbeing technology, if the venue accepts design frameworks.

## Critical Evidence Gaps

| Gap | Why it matters | Required work before stronger claims |
|---|---|---|
| No user study of Pains & Gains / HelpPS yet | Cannot claim user benefit or harm reduction | Usability study, qualitative safety review, prototype evaluation |
| No validated classifier performance data | Cannot claim safe routing accuracy | Multilingual red-team dataset, expert labels, recall/false-negative evaluation |
| No clinical or DV advisor validation yet | Framework may miss subtle coercion or unsafe phrasing | Advisor review, scenario testing, harm analysis |
| No KSA cultural validation yet | Arabic and KSA deployment cannot be direct translation | Arabic-language UX research, cultural advisor review, resource verification |
| No production privacy architecture | Local storage is acceptable for prototype only | Server-side gateway, consent ledger, retention policy, data residency design |
| No longitudinal outcome data | Cannot prove relief, load reduction, or graduation | Pilot study with validated outcome measures and safety endpoints |
